# Sample After-Sales Policy

> This is a self-written demo document for RAG testing and project demonstration.

## 1. Scope

This policy describes after-sales handling for demo e-commerce orders, including returns, delayed delivery, damaged products, wrong items, refunds, and complaint escalation.

## 2. Return Handling

Customers may request a return when the item is unused, within the allowed return window, and belongs to a returnable product category. Support should verify order status, delivery date, product category, and prior after-sales records.

Return requests should be approved when the request is within the return window, the product is eligible, the item is not marked as final sale, and required photos or explanations are provided for condition-based claims.

## 3. Delayed Delivery

Delayed delivery occurs when the actual delivery date is later than the promised delivery date. Support should check carrier status, region, product availability, and previous delay signals.

Recommended actions include updated delivery information, a small coupon for moderate delay, escalation when delay exceeds seven days, and regional operations review for repeated delays.

## 4. Product Damage

If a customer reports damaged goods, support should request photos, check packaging notes, and create an after-sales record with type `product_damage`.

Product damage cases should be escalated when the product belongs to electronics, baby, health, or food, when multiple damage reports appear for the same SKU, or when the customer provides evidence of safety risk.

## 5. Wrong Item

Wrong item cases require comparing ordered SKU, shipped SKU, and warehouse batch if available. The support team may arrange replacement or refund based on inventory.

Wrong item cases should be audited because they can indicate warehouse process issues.

## 6. Refund Handling

Refunds should be processed after policy checks and required approvals. High-value refunds, repeated refund requests, and manual overrides require human review.

Refund communication generated by the AI Agent must remain a draft until approved.

## 7. Complaint Escalation

Customer complaints are escalated when the customer reports repeated unresolved problems, low satisfaction, regulatory risk, safety concerns, or high-value loss. The escalation record should include order ID, issue type, proposed resolution, owner, and due date.
